BUFFALO CAULIFLOWER – EREWHON COPYCAT

Buffalo cauliflower is the ultimate game-changer if you’re looking for a healthier, veggie-packed alternative to your favorite buffalo wings. This recipe is a delicious copycat of Erewhon’s famous buffalo cauliflower, delivering all the spicy, tangy goodness you crave with a satisfying crunch. Perfect as an appetizer, snack, or even a main dish, this buffalo cauliflower is sure to be a hit at your next gathering.

MAKING THE CAULIFLOWER

Start by preheating your oven to 425°F to 450°F depending on your oven’s strength. While that’s heating up, get your cauliflower ready by cutting it into bite-sized florets. You want them to be just the right size so they bake evenly and get nice and crispy.

Next, let’s make the batter. In a mixing bowl, combine buttermilk, cornstarch, flour, chili powder, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Whisk it all together until you have a thick, smooth batter. This is the secret to getting that perfect crunchy coating on your cauliflower.

Now, toss your cauliflower florets into the batter, making sure each piece is thoroughly coated. You can either buy a bag of cauliflower florets or buy a whole head and chop the florets off yourself. Once your florets are coated, add the panko breadcrumbs and give everything a good mix. The panko will add an extra layer of crunch that takes this dish to the next level.

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and spread out the cauliflower, making sure the pieces aren’t touching. This ensures they bake evenly and get crispy all around. Pop them in the oven for about 20 minutes. Take it out of the oven, spray with avocado oil, and pop back in the oven for 10 to 15 more minutes.

While the cauliflower is baking, it’s time to prepare your buffalo sauce. You can use store-bought buffalo sauce or make your own if you’re feeling adventurous. My favorite buffalo sauces are Sweet Baby Ray’s and Frank’s Red Hot. Once the cauliflower is crispy and golden, take it out of the oven, transfer it to a bowl, and toss it in the buffalo sauce.

After the cauliflower is nicely coated in buffalo sauce, put it back on the baking sheet and bake for an additional 10 to 15 minutes until golden and crispy. This final bake helps the sauce adhere to the cauliflower and ensures every bite is packed with flavor.

Once it’s done, take it out of the oven and sprinkle some chopped chives on top for a pop of color and a little extra flavor. Serve your buffalo cauliflower with a side of ranch or blue cheese dressing for dipping, and enjoy!

TIPS AND TRICKS:

  • If you’re short on buttermilk, you can make a quick substitut by adding a tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to regular milk and letting it sit for a few minutes.
  • To make this dish gluten-free, simply swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend and use gluten-free panko breadcrumbs.
  • For a vegan version, use a plant-based milk alternative and a vegan buffalo sauce.

VARIATIONS:

  • Try different seasonings in the batter, like cumin or smoked paprika, to change up the flavor profile.
  • Add some grated Parmesan cheese to the panko breadcrumbs for a cheesy twist.
  • If you prefer a milder dish, use a less spicy hot sauce or dilute your buffalo sauce with a bit of melted butter. You could also mix a bit of ranch with buffalo sauce to make it more mild and creamy.

This buffalo cauliflower recipe is a versatile and delicious dish that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re serving it up for a party, a family dinner, or just a tasty snack, it’s guaranteed to impress. Enjoy!

BUFFALO CAULIFLOWER – EREWHON COPYCAT

Craving a delicious and healthier alternative to buffalo wings? Try this Erewhon copycat recipe for buffalo cauliflower that perfectly balances spicy, tangy flavors with a satisfying crunch. This easy-to-make dish is perfect for game days, appetizers, or just a tasty snack that everyone will love. And it all comes together in the oven!

Print Recipe

PREP TIME: 15 MINUTES

COOK TIME: 45 MINUTES

TOTAL TIME: 1 HOUR

YIELDS: 4

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 whole head cauliflower, florets cut off
  • 1 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/3 cup cornstarch
  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/3 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • avocado oil spray
  • 1/2 cup buffalo sauce, more as needed
  • chopped chives, optional garnish

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.

  2. To a large mixing bowl, add the buttermilk, cornstarch, flour, chili powder, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Whisk together until you have a thick batter consistency.

  3. Add the cauliflower florets and mix until all are coated evenly.

  4. Then add the panko breadcrumbs and mix again.

  5. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and add the battered cauliflower so that they're not touching.

  6. Pop in the oven to bake for 20 minutes.

  7. Remove from the oven. Flip over each floret, spray with oil, and then bake for an additional 10 to 15 minutes until golden and crispy all around.

  8. Once golden and crispy, take it out of the oven. Add to a bow and add as little or as much buffalo sauce as you'd like and toss it.

  9. Transfer back to the tray and bake for an additional 10 to 15 minutes.

  10. Remove from the oven, top it off with chopped chives, and enjoy with some ranch or blue cheese dressing.
